"I found myself questioning whether musical theater was really the best place for me. My voice coach at that time, Mona Greenberg of Manalapan, had introduced me to some classical soprano pieces, which she felt would highlight my strengths. Those pieces, sung in Italian, grew on me, and friends and family also began to notice how well they suited my voice. I began to realize that even within musical theater the songs I loved the most were what I consider “legit styled” works, such as Christine from Phantom of the Opera. Then, with little time to spare, I let my parents know that I decided to apply to programs in classical vocal performance instead."

As a recent graduate of Mason Gross School of the Arts Rutgers University, Siri has studied Vocal Performance (B.M.). Previous engagements with the New York Lyric Opera include the role of Susanna (Le nozze di Figaro), Barbarina (Le nozze di Figaro), and teaching private singing lessons as an apprentice teacher with the New York Singing Teachers' Association. She was also a finalist in the Beijing "Shui Li Fang Bei" Mandarin pop singing competition in 2015, which can be viewed on her website www.selenasiri.com or Youtube along with other recent performances. In addition to her music, she continues to search for new ways to display "truthful, living art" along with technology. She is currently pursuing courses in HTML, CSS and Javascript alongside singing to reach a wider audience and improve upon web designing.

In addition to voice performance, Siri has been teaching private singing lessons as an apprentice teacher with the New York Singing Teachers' Association. This has taken place since October 2018. She strongly believes that in order to hold strong teaching skills a teacher must whole-heartedly comprehend the knowledge they are handing down to the student. Just as a student who is furtherdown the road of education, she has gained an immense amount of satisfaction from continuing her studies on how to properly teach the voice. This was through vocal pedagogy courses that overlaped topics in anatomy and physiology, psychology, and social pressures that can all influence one’s sound quality. On the side, Siri is passionate about animal rights and is an activist for Anonymous for the Voiceless. The organization was a movement promoting a vegan diet to end world suffering. She also participates with the Curvy Girls group that stands up for those living with scoliosis. She looks forward to continuing her journey with activism and helping others find their voice. It is her goal to use all these possible ways of communication to continue influencing her students and community for deeper understandings and a better lifestyle.
